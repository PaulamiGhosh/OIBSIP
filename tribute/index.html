Only Reason of Our Freedom

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

Born

Subhas Chandra Bose was born in 23 January in 1897, at Cuttack in Odisha. His father,Janakinath Bose was a famous lawyer. His mother, Prabhavati Devi was a housewife. Subhas was the ninth child of their parents.

Education

1902- He studied in Protestant European School
1902 - 1913- He shifted to Ravenshaw Collegiate School.
1913 - 1916- He took admission in Presidency College, Culcutta. But due to Nationalist activities he was expelled from that college.
1916 - 1918- He continued his graduation at Scottish Church College, under University of Culcutta.
1919 - He got admission in Fitzwilliam College under Universsity of Cambridge to continue his ICS training.

Achievements

He ranked Second in the Matriculation Examination
He secured First class Second in B.A. with Honours in Philosophy
He got Fourth rank in the ICS, the toughest examination

Early Life In Congress

  • Though he bagged the 4th place in the examination, he refused to work under the British government.

  • After hearing the turmoil in India, he came back and started the Swaraj newspaper for the promotion of the Bengal Provincial Congress Committee.

  • Netaji joined the non-cooperation movement begun by Mahatma Gandhi. Netaji was advised by Gandhiji to work with Chitta Ranjan Das, who was a politician in Bengal. Thereafter Netaji became a journalist, and commandant of the Bengal Congress volunteers.

  • In 1924, Netaji was appointed as the chief executive officer of the Calcutta Municipal Corporation, and Das as a mayor. Netaji was then deported to Myanmar since he was suspected of connections with secret revolutionary movements.

  • In 1927, after Netaji’s return from Burma, he found Bengal Congress affairs in disarray after the death of Das, and was elected as the president of the Bengal Congress. Soon Netaji and Jawaharlal Nehru became two general secretaries of the Indian National Congress.

  • In 1938, Netaji was elected as president of the Indian National Congress. He then formulated a policy of broad industrialisation which didn’t fall in sync with the Gandhian economic thought.

After Leaving Congress

  • Netaji left the Congress leadership positions in 1939 due to the differences between Mahatma Gandhi and the Congress high command.
  • On January 26, 1941, Netaji escaped from Kolkata residence and, travelled via Kabul and Moscow, and reached Germany in April.
  • In July 1943, at a meeting in Singapore, Rash Behari Bose handed over control of the organisation of Indian Independence League to Subhas Chandra Bose. He then changed the name into Azad Hind Fauj
  • In Germany, Bose came under the protection of a newly created Special Bureau for India. Netaji and other Indians in Berlin made regular broadcasts in different languages from the Azad Hind Radio sponsored by Germany. Through the broadcasts, he urged his countrymen to rise against the British rule.

Success of AZAD HIND FAUJ

On October 21, 1943, Bose proclaimed the establishment of Azad Hind Fauj, alongside Japanese troops, advanced to Rangoon and thence overland into India, reaching Indian soil on March 18, 1944, at Andaman and Nicobar islands and were renamed as Shaheed and Swaraj by him and moving into Kohima and the plains of Imphal. On April 14, 1944, the Azad Hind Fauj captured Moirang in Manipur and half of Nagaland from the clutches of the British Empire. It was a fierce battle and one of the most difficult ever faced by the British forces in India, as admitted by the Britishers themselves. In a stubborn battle, the mixed Indian and Japanese forces, lacking Japanese air support, were defeated and forced to retreat; the Azad Hind Fauj nevertheless for some time succeeded in maintaining its identity as a liberation army, based in Burma and then Indochina.
